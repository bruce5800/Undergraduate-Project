\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\textbf{LLM serving systems.}
Orca~\cite{yu2022orca} introduced iteration-level continuous
batching, vLLM~\cite{kwon2023vllm} added PagedAttention
for non-contiguous KV allocation
(2--4$\times$ throughput), and
Sarathi-Serve~\cite{agrawal2024sarathi} introduced chunked
prefill to mitigate generation stalls in mixed batches. These
optimize \emph{within} a single server's GPU and are
\textbf{orthogonal} to our cross-server scheduling: our
admission-time batching is a tractable abstraction of their
iteration-level batching, and our RL's per-request placement
decisions would propagate into vLLM-style engines unchanged.

\textbf{Cluster scheduling.}
HEFT~\cite{topcuoglu2002heft} computes upward-rank task
priorities for static DAG assignment; genetic
algorithms~\cite{holland1975adaptation} and
PSO~\cite{kennedy1995pso} apply population-based search to
scheduling fitness landscapes; industry uses simpler
load-balancing primitives (Round-Robin, Least-Loaded,
Shortest-Queue). We compare against all of these in
\S\ref{sec:eval}; none natively encodes AIGC physics.

\textbf{RL for scheduling.}
Decima~\cite{mao2019decima} uses a GNN over Spark DAG
structure with REINFORCE; RLScheduler~\cite{zhang2020rlscheduler}
applies RL to HPC batch jobs;
A3C-R2N2~\cite{tuli2022a3cr2n} uses asynchronous
actor-critic with a residual recurrent encoder for edge-cloud
IoT tasks---the closest predecessor in deployment topology
but predating the AIGC era. We benchmark A3C-R2N2 as a
\textbf{controlled baseline} (\S\ref{sec:main-results}):
holding actor-critic constant isolates the AIGC contribution
of our state and reward design ($+28\%$ \slo, $-7\%$ \etok,
$p<0.05$). We also implement a Decima-style GNN variant and
find that, under realistic training budgets, a feedforward
MLP with explicit AIGC features matches or exceeds the GNN's
relational reasoning---a departure from Decima's original
Spark-DAG findings.

\textbf{AIGC-specific scheduling.} Splitwise, DistServe, and
Llumnix~\cite{patel2024splitwise,zhong2024distserve,sun2024llumnix}
(\S\ref{sec:bg-cloudedge}) design \emph{deployment topologies}
(phase splitting, disaggregated pools) and
\emph{intra-cluster reactive policies} (mid-flight
migration), treating initial request placement as orthogonal.
\textbf{Our work attacks the initial placement problem}: the
two strands compose---Llumnix-style migration could refine
our placements at runtime; our scheduler could populate
prefill/decode pools in a Splitwise-style deployment.

\textbf{Simulation platforms.}
CloudSim~\cite{calheiros2011cloudsim},
iFogSim~\cite{gupta2017ifogsim}, and
WorkflowSim~\cite{chen2012workflowsim} target general
cloud-fog or DAG workloads without AIGC physics; recent
LLM-serving simulators model intra-server batching dynamics
rather than cross-server scheduling. To our knowledge our
simulator (\S\ref{sec:simulator}) is the first open-source
platform combining all five AIGC physics, cross-server
scheduling focus, and native multi-objective (\slo{}~+~energy)
evaluation. We release it with reproducibility manifests for
the 30+ configurations in \S\ref{sec:eval}.
